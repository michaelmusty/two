# Research workspace — 2-group Belyi maps × automated theorem provers

## Thesis (Musty, Dartmouth 2019): "2-Group Belyi Maps"
Explicit computation of Galois Belyi maps φ: X → P¹ with 2-group monodromy.
- **Ch.3** — algorithm (Magma) enumerating isomorphism classes of 2-group Belyi maps
  = transitive permutation triples (σ₀,σ₁,σ∞) generating a 2-group, up to simultaneous
  conjugation. Counts by degree (Thm 1.2.2):
  | d | 1 | 2 | 4 | 8 | 16 | 32 | 64 | 128 | 256 |
  |---|---|---|---|---|----|----|----|-----|-----|
  | # |1|3|7|19|55|151|503|1799|7175|
- **Ch.4** — Thm 1.2.4: every 2-group Belyi map of degree ≤256 is defined over a
  quadratic extension of an abelian number field ramified only at 2.
- **Ch.5** — algorithms to compute explicit equations (over F_q, p≠2, and char 0 / Q̄).

## Open questions (the research targets)
- **Q1.2.5** Are all 2-group Belyi maps defined over *solvable* number fields? Is the
  field generated by the 2-power torsion of Jac(X) solvable over Q?
- **Q6.2.1** Are the moduli fields of 2-group Belyi maps always solvable?
- Stakes: a *nonsolvable* moduli/torsion field would be a nonsolvable number field
  ramified only at 2 (cf. Dembélé 2009, [15]). Roberts supplied a non-Galois triple of
  degree 2²⁰, refined-passport size 16 (eq. 6.2.2) as a candidate test case.
