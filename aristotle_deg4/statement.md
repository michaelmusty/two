# Generating-pair counts underlying degree-4 two-group Belyi maps

For a finite group `G`, call an ordered pair `(a, b) ∈ G × G` a **generating pair**
if the subgroup generated by `{a, b}` is the whole group `G` (i.e. `⊤`).

Formalize the following two statements in Lean 4 (with Mathlib) and prove them.
Finite enumeration / `decide` over the finite group is acceptable.

**Theorem 1.** The cyclic group `ZMod 4` has exactly `12` generating pairs:
the finite set `{ (a, b) : ZMod 4 × ZMod 4 | ⟨{a, b}⟩ = ⊤ }` has cardinality `12`.

**Theorem 2.** The Klein four-group `ZMod 2 × ZMod 2` has exactly `6` generating pairs:
the finite set `{ (a, b) : (ZMod 2 × ZMod 2)² | ⟨{a, b}⟩ = ⊤ }` has cardinality `6`.

(Context: with `|Aut(ZMod 4)| = 2` and `|Aut(V4)| = 6`, these give
`12/2 + 6/6 = 7` isomorphism classes of degree-4 two-group Belyi maps — the `d = 4`
entry of Theorem 1.2.2 in Musty's thesis. Note `ZMod 4` and `ZMod 2 × ZMod 2` are
additive groups, so use `AddSubgroup` / `AddSubgroup.closure`.)
